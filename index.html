About this project

An externship deliverable exploring how personality data behaves at two scales: the microscopic (where any given character lands among thousands) and the macroscopic (what the personality-content market on YouTube actually looks like, and what kinds of videos win attention).

Both visualizations are built around the same conviction — that good data viz lets a human, with limited working memory, understand a system more complex than they could hold in their head. The character map collapses 500-dimensional trait data into three named axes; the YouTube galaxy uses non-linear embedding to surface natural content clusters that the underlying language predicts but a person reading titles couldn't see.
